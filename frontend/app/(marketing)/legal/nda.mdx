# Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

**Last Updated:** July 7, 2026

---

## 1. Parties

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("**NDA**") is entered into by and between:

**SAQYN RABT** ("**The Company**"), a company registered in the **State of Qatar** under Commercial Registration (CR) number [●], with its principal place of business at [●], Qatar; and

**The Client** ("**The Counterparty**"), the individual or entity executing this Agreement with SAQYN RABT (collectively referred to as the "**Parties**" and individually as a "**Party**").

Each Party may act as a "**Disclosing Party**" or "**Receiving Party**" depending on which Party discloses Confidential Information to the other.

---

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

### 2.1 Confidential Information of the Client

For the Client (as Disclosing Party), "**Confidential Information**" includes, but is not limited to:

- All HR policies, employee handbooks, and personnel records (including medical and vacation records)
- Financial data, revenue figures, and banking information
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) and internal business processes
- Business strategies, expansion plans, and competitive analyses
- Customer data, client lists, and vendor information imported into the SAQYN RABT dashboard
- Any documents, files, or materials uploaded to the SAQYN RABT platform

### 2.2 Confidential Information of SAQYN RABT

For SAQYN RABT (as Disclosing Party), "**Confidential Information**" includes, but is not limited to:

- The underlying source code, software architecture, and technical documentation
- AI prompt engineering protocols, system instructions, and model configurations
- System architecture blueprints, database schemas, and infrastructure design
- Pricing models, discount structures, and revenue data
- Customer database, sales strategies, and business development plans
- Any proprietary algorithms, methodologies, or trade secrets

### 2.3 Form of Confidential Information

Confidential Information may be disclosed in written, oral, visual, or electronic form and must be:

- **If in written or tangible form:** Clearly marked as "Confidential" or "Proprietary" at the time of disclosure; or
- **If disclosed orally or visually:** Identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and summarized in writing within 30 days.

---

## 3. Obligations of the Receiving Party

### 3.1 Duty of Confidentiality

The Receiving Party agrees to hold all Confidential Information in **strict confidence** and to exercise the same degree of care to protect it as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care, in accordance with **Article 172 of the Qatari Civil Code (Law No. 22 of 2004)** requiring good faith in contractual performance.

### 3.2 Limited Use

The Receiving Party shall not use the Confidential Information for any purpose other than:

- Evaluating a potential business relationship between the Parties
- Executing the existing Terms of Service or Service Agreement between the Parties

### 3.3 Access Restriction

The Receiving Party shall restrict access to the Confidential Information to only those employees, agents, or contractors who:

- Have a legitimate business need to know such information
- Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained in this NDA

### 3.4 No Reverse Engineering

The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or attempt to derive the source code, underlying algorithms, or structure of any Confidential Information received in machine-readable or executable form.

---

## 4. Exclusions

Confidential Information **does not include** information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written evidence:

- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party and without breach of this NDA
- Was in the Receiving Party's lawful possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as proven by written records
- Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as proven by written records
- Is rightfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party who has the lawful right to disclose it without restriction

### 4.1 Required Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is required by applicable law, regulation, or valid court order to disclose Confidential Information, it shall:

- Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in advance (unless legally prohibited)
- Cooperate with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy
- Disclose only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required to comply

---

## 5. Term and Termination

### 5.1 Effective Date

This NDA shall commence upon the earlier of:

- The execution of this Agreement by both Parties; or
- The commencement of discussions or disclosure of Confidential Information between the Parties

### 5.2 Duration

This NDA shall survive the termination of any underlying service agreement or business relationship between the Parties for a period of **three (3) years**.

### 5.3 Survival of Obligations

The obligations of confidentiality and non-use under this NDA shall continue for a period of **three (3) years** from the date of last disclosure of Confidential Information, even if the business relationship between the Parties has been terminated.

---

## 6. Return of Materials

### 6.1 Obligation to Return or Destroy

Upon the termination of the business relationship, or upon the Disclosing Party's written request, the Receiving Party shall, at its option:

- Return all copies of the Confidential Information in any form to the Disclosing Party; or
- Destroy all copies of the Confidential Information in its possession, including all electronic copies and backups

### 6.2 Certification

The Receiving Party shall provide written certification to the Disclosing Party within **30 days** of such request confirming that all Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed in accordance with this Section.

### 6.3 Retention for Legal Compliance

Notwithstanding the above, the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information as required by applicable law, regulation, or bona fide document retention policies, provided that such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this NDA.

---

## 7. No License or Ownership

Nothing in this NDA grants either Party any license, right, or interest in or to the Confidential Information of the other Party. All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party. This NDA does not grant the Receiving Party any right to use the Disclosing Party's trademarks, patents, copyrights, or other intellectual property.

---

## 8. No Warranty

All Confidential Information is provided "**AS IS**" and without any warranty, express or implied, regarding its accuracy, completeness, or fitness for a particular purpose. Neither Party shall be liable for any loss or damage arising from the use of Confidential Information by the Receiving Party.

---

## 9. Remedies

### 9.1 Irreparable Harm

The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this NDA may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy.

### 9.2 Injunctive Relief

In the event of a breach or threatened breach of this NDA, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek **injunctive relief** and specific performance in addition to any other remedies available at law or equity, without the requirement of posting a bond, in accordance with the principles of the **Qatari Civil Code (Law No. 22 of 2004)**.

---

## 10. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

### 10.1 Governing Law

> **This NDA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Qatar, without giving effect to any conflict-of-law provisions, including the Qatari Civil Code (Law No. 22 of 2004) and the Law on Electronic Commerce and Transactions (Law No. 8 of 2019).**

### 10.2 Jurisdiction

> **Any dispute arising under or relating to this NDA shall be resolved exclusively in the competent courts of Doha, Qatar.**

### 10.3 Alternative Dispute Resolution

The parties may, by mutual written agreement, elect to submit disputes to arbitration under the rules of the **Qatar International Court and Dispute Resolution Centre (QICDRC)** .

---

## 11. Miscellaneous

### 11.1 Entire Agreement

This NDA constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and communications, whether written or oral.

### 11.2 Amendments

No amendment or modification of this NDA shall be valid unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

### 11.3 Waiver

No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this NDA shall constitute a waiver of that right.

### 11.4 Severability

If any provision of this NDA is held to be invalid or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect in accordance with Article 174 of the Qatari Civil Code.

### 11.5 Counterparts

This NDA may be executed in counterparts (including electronic signatures under Law No. 8 of 2019), each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
